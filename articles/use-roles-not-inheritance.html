Use Roles, Not Inheritance

Every once in a while I write a long answer on Quora and it takes off. My answer explaining why inheritance in Object-Oriented (OO) programming is a bad idea1 was one of those answers. At 37,000 views and over 400 upvotes, it wasn't hugely successful, but periodically I get an email from Quora letting me know that it's been found by software developers, yet again, and given a spate of upvotes. Unfortunately, I didn't take much time to explain the solution to this problem, so that's what this little article is about.

I won't belabor my points about inheritance because I covered them in the Quora answer and, if you've been working with OO code long enough, you should be well famliar with the problems. That being said, I would like to remind you, again, that Alan Kay, the inventor of the term “object-oriented programming”, had serious reservations about inheritance2 and eventually he left inheritance out of the definition of OO programming.3
